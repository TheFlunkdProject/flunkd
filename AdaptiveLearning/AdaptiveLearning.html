What is adaptive learning?

Previous to the advent of the internet, a system that implemented adaptive learning in any practical way would have been impossible to create. Students have had to adapt themselves (to the extent possible) to the way the teacher chose to present material. Adaptive learning adjusts the lesson to the student.

If each of us had a private tutor that understood our needs and likes, we could learn at a much faster pace than we could in a classroom. It's not that we each think and learn in such radical ways that it's impossible to track; it's just that when you put a few dozen of us together and try to teach us all in the same way, things get very difficult for everyone. Difficulty can sometimes be a motivator to try harder, but in this case it clouds our minds and smothers our curiosity. Curiosity is the most powerful way to motivate the mind. Adaptive learning will eventually clear the path for curiosity to work its wonder.

The difference between what Khan Academe is talking about and what the world needs right now is that the focus needs to be on the experience of the student - not the result of the lesson. Students can feel when they are learning, and they know what they need better than any efficiency algorithm. Basing the student's struggle on results is only an attempt to solve the problem indirectly.

The solution will actually be a combination of these two approaches. But this approach will need to be the foundation on which the other approach builds. Making a funner learning experience for students will attract more students and will spread faster than focusing only on results. We aim to create a Pandora-like approach to education. But we need help spreading this idea so that more people want to try it.

Once people begin to see the power behind adaptive learning it will begin to be widely adopted, and all kinds of approaches will be experimented with. We will get closer and closer to a perfect solution that will have 14 year-olds earning college degrees.
