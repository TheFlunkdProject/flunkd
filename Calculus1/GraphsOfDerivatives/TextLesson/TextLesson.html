You need to use the meaning of the derivative to figure these out. The derivative is the slope. The slope of one function becomes the y value of its derivative. So, the slope of f at a place will be the y-coordinate of at the same place, and the slope of at a place will be the y-coordinate of at the same place.

In our graph, I would look at the place on the right where the green curve has a horizontal tangent (slope=0). I would expect green's derivative to cross zero at that same x-value. And it looks like both blue and red do. So I know that either red is the derivative of green, or blue is. On the other side where blue has a horizontal tangent line, its derivative should be zero. Both green and red cross zero. So either green is the derivative of blue, or red is.

So the information points to either original=green, first derivative=blue, second derivative=red, or original=green, first derivative=red, second derivative=blue. Let's make a guess and check it at some random point. Look at x=-2. Let's say green is the original. It's slope looks like it's about negative two. So is the position of one of the other lines at y=-2? Blue is down there. Now, what is the slope of blue? It likes like 1.5 ish. Is red at y=1.5? It's about there. So the answer is: =green, =blue, and =red.

It's a strange concept to visualize at first, but it gets easier. Just have patience and be very deliberate at first. There are many ways to come up with the answer if you analyze the problem long enough.

It easiest to use significant features of one to look at the others. Find places where the slope is zero on one curve, and look for other curves that cross the x-axis at the same place. If there are any, then they are probably the derivative of the curve with the zero slope.

Final note: The derivative of a function does not take into consideration the height of the function, but only its shape The value of a slope of a line has nothing to do with how high the line is above the x-axis. So the derivative will not care what the vertical positioning of the original curve was, since it is a graph of the values of the slopes. .
