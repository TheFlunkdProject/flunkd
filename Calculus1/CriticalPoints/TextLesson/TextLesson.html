3.11 - Critical Points

Critical points are all the points where the derivative of a function is either undefined, or zero, and in the domain of the original function. To find them, follow this process:

  1. Take the derivative
  2. Find where the derivative equals zero or is undefined.
  3. Check that it is in the domain of the original function.
  4. Find the y coordinate of the point (because it is a critical point).

Critical points have an extremely important application. You can use them to find extreme values of a function minima and maxima. When a function reaches a peak, it hits its maximum value, and right where it is at its highest point, the slope is zero. That's why critical points can be used to find minima and maxima.
