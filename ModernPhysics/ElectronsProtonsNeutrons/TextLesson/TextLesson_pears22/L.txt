J. J. Thompson discovered the first subatomic particle, the electron, in 1897
<BR>
<BR>In 1911 Ernest Rutherford discovered, through scattering experiments, that atoms were similar in structure to planetary systems. He discovered the nucleus, and that electrons move far away around it. By 1919 he had shown that the nucleus could also be divided, and that one of the particles that flies off is the hydrogen nucleus, and he called it the "proton." ("first one")
